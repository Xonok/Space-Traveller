        How this all works:
        The growth and migration values shown are what happened last tick.
        Industries grow when they consume inputs.
        They do so every 3 hours ("tick").
        Growth: with sufficient inputs, population grows.
        Migration: When inputs are very abundant, population can grow very fast.
        Very scarce inputs cause population to decrease, both through growth and migration.

        There are 4 kinds of industries:
         *Primary - Creates output items if any inputs are met. Workers increase or decrease depending on availability of inputs.
        *Secondary - Creates output items if all inputs are met. Workers don't increase when even one input is missing.
        *Tertiary - Produces credits based on the value of consumed items. Worker amount is limited to all non-tertiary industries summed up.
        *Special - Doesn't produce items or credits.
        Fighting, trading, using factories gives xp.
        Every 1000 xp gives a level and 1 skillpoint.
        Skillpoints can be used to train skills,
        which are mostly needed to equip or use items.
        Some valuable or high-level skills require items to train them,
        usually there's an alternative location that doesn't.
        Tech 0 items and ships are always usable regardless of skills.
